## What the evidence actually shows

Three things follow from that table:

**1. "Trailing ~1mo" is one day of data.** `drag_month` is `sum(hist[-20:])` — a cumulative sum over a 20-session window, not a mean. The window currently holds two entries, and 2026-07-15 contributed 0.0 (that session had zero fills). So the entire 113.3 bps was generated by a single session, today, and the "month" framing in the alarm text is an artifact of a window that has not filled yet. A steady state anywhere near today's daily figure would breach a 30 bps *cumulative* band within one session regardless of execution quality.

**2. The real anomaly is the median slippage, and it is not what fired.** Median fill slipped **331.99 bps** against the reference close across 16 fills. `BANDS["stock_bps"]` is `(0.0, 15.0)`. That is roughly 22x the stock band, and it is the number I would want a Coordinator looking at — MC-DRAG is arguably just the downstream symptom of it.

**3. MC-POSITION-GAP is benign and can probably be dismissed.** The $350 is four legs off by exactly one share each (ALB −1, GLW −1, HPE −1, WBD +1) against `round(target_dollars / ref_close)`. That is integer rounding on a small-notional book, not drift.

## Why this is probably a measurement question, not a trading failure

Context: `momentum_concentrated` was cut over into its own dedicated Alpaca account on **2026-07-15** (`memos/mc-account-isolation-cutover-2026-07-15.md`). Yesterday was the empty account (0 fills, $6,118 gap); today's 16 fills are the **initial build-out** of the book into it, and the sleeve is still only ~42% established.

**The band appears to have been inherited unchanged across an architecture change that redefined what it measures.** Per the cutover memo, `book_drag_bps_month = 30` was calibrated when per-book drag was **pro-rated from a shared account trading liquid ETFs** (`etf_bps` band: 0-5). MC now trades **single names in a dedicated account with exact attribution** (`stock_bps` band: 0-15). Same threshold, structurally different and more expensive quantity. That alone could explain a breach without anything being wrong.

That does not explain 332 bps. Candidate hypotheses, explicitly **unverified** — I did not test any of these, and each is falsifiable by someone with write access:

- **Reference-price mismatch (most likely).** The runner fires 20:30 PT, after the close; fills land at the next open. If `closes` supplies the *prior* close as the reference for a fill that executed at the *next* open, the overnight gap is booked as slippage. That would inflate every fill roughly in one direction and is consistent with a high positive median.
- **Stale reference during cutover.** If the `closes` reference is misdated relative to the fill date across the 7/15→7/16 transition, apparent slippage inflates without any real cost.
- **Genuine build-out cost.** Possible, but a 332 bps median across 16 liquid names strains it. I would rank this last.

## What I am asking for

Nothing automated. Specifically **not** proposing a band change — widening a threshold to silence an alarm that may be correctly detecting a broken reference price is the wrong order of operations.

1. **Rule on whether the 332 bps median is real cost or a reference-price artifact.** This is the load-bearing question; MC-DRAG follows from it either way.
2. **If artifact:** the fix belongs in the reconcile's reference-price selection (`reconcile_mc_date`'s `closes` argument), which is Coordinator-owned.
3. **If real:** MC-DRAG is doing its job and the build-out cost needs a decision on its own terms.
4. **Separately, decide whether `book_drag_bps_month = 30` is still the right band** for a dedicated single-name account, given it was set for pro-rated ETF aggregation. Worth an explicit ruling either way rather than silent inheritance.
5. **Consider whether the alarm text should stop saying "trailing ~1mo"** until the window has ≥N entries. Today it reports one session as a month, which is what made this look worse than it may be at first read.
